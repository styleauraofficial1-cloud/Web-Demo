Case Studies

Expandable UX breakdowns for each concept project.

Each study explains the problem, target users, UX goals, design decisions, flow, value, and skills used.

Overview

A reusable landing page system for small local service businesses that need a polished first impression and a clear inquiry path.

Problem

Outdated pages often hide services, bury contact details, and fail on mobile, causing visitors to leave before taking action.

Target Users

Local customers comparing providers on mobile, business owners who need simple lead handling, and first-time visitors looking for trust signals.

UX Goals

Clarify the offer, make the primary CTA visible, reduce page friction, and give the business an easy contact/booking flow.

Design Solution

Hero with strong CTA, service cards, credibility blocks, FAQ section, contact form, and responsive sections that guide scanning.

Key Screens / Sections

Hero, service overview, proof block, FAQ widget, contact form, and mobile CTA sequence.

User Flow

Visitor lands on hero, scans services, checks proof, reads FAQ, then submits a request or opens WhatsApp/email handoff.

UI Decisions

Large headings, card hierarchy, repeated CTA placement, strong spacing, accessible contrast, and short UX writing.

Outcome / Value

A business owner gets a cleaner presentation and visitors get a faster path from interest to inquiry.

Skills Used

UI/UX design, responsive layout, conversion-focused design, UX writing, form planning, and HTML/CSS implementation.

Overview

A small website widget concept that answers common questions and guides users toward contact or booking.

Website visitors, local service customers, business owners, and support/admin teams.
